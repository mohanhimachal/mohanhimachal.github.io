About

Three decades. One mission.

For thirty years, Mohan Sharma has taught mathematics in the schools of Himachal Pradesh — first as a young TGT at Jawahar Navodaya Vidyalaya in 1995, and today as Principal of Government Girls Senior Secondary School Indora. The classrooms have changed. The mission hasn't: bring the best of mathematics, technology, and global thinking to students who would otherwise never encounter them.

Along the way: a Fulbright Fellowship to the University of North Carolina Wilmington in 2020. A seat on the NCERT National Mathematics Syllabi Review Committee. A State Teacher Award from the Himachal Pradesh Government in 2024 for ICT integration and teacher training. Four international project grants funding student-led work on technical skills, environmental awareness, women's safety, and herbal medicine. More than 6,700 teachers trained as a resource person under SSA, RMSA, NISHTHA, NIPUN, and STARS programs.

What ties it together is a quiet conviction: a girl in a rural school in Kangra deserves the same quality of mathematics, the same exposure to technology, and the same belief in her own capability as any student anywhere in the world. The rest is just the work of making that true.

My students don't need to leave Kangra to think globally. They just need someone to open the door.
